Paper Review - Dissecting the Performance of Strongly-Consistent Replication Protocols

Consensus protocols perform differently due to different network, workload, and deploy- ment conditions. This paper devoted to make a comparison in LANs and WANs between single-leader, multi-leader, hierarchical multi-leader, and leaderless (opportunistic leader) consensus protocols.

To model consensus performance, the paper estimates the average latency.
latency = wQ + μ + DL + DQ
latency → total time that takes client to reach consensus
wQ → round's queue waiting time
μ → How long it takes the leader to process all messages for a given round
DL → network delays between client and leader
DQ → network delays between leader and follower in a quorum
